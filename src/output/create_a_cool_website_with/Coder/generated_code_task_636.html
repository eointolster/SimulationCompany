Monkey of the Month: Golden Snub-nosed Monkey

The Golden Snub-nosed Monkey lives in the mountains of central and southwestern China. With their vibrant golden fur and distinctive blue faces, these endangered primates are among the world's most striking monkey species.

  • Survives in freezing temperatures unlike most monkeys
  • Lives in groups of up to 600 individuals
  • Has a diet of lichen, bark, and seasonal fruits

Conservation Corner

Learn about efforts to protect monkey species around the world

Protecting Our Primate Cousins

Over 60% of the world's primate species are threatened with extinction. Conservation efforts are crucial to ensure these intelligent animals continue to thrive in their natural habitats.

Conservation Success Stories

Golden Lion Tamarin Recovery

Once down to fewer than 200 individuals in the wild, this distinctive orange monkey now has a population of over 3,500 thanks to dedicated conservation efforts in Brazil.

Mountain Gorilla Population Growth

Though not technically monkeys, these great apes represent an important conservation success, with numbers increasing from 620 to over 1,000 in the past decade.

How You Can Help

  • "Adopt" a monkey through a reputable conservation organization
  • Support sustainable products that don't destroy monkey habitats
  • Spread awareness about endangered monkey species
  • Visit responsible primate sanctuaries and reserves
