Featured Tree of the Month

Sugar Maple (Acer saccharum)

This iconic North American deciduous tree is known for its brilliant fall colors and as the source of maple syrup.

  • Height: 60-75 feet (18-23m)
  • Lifespan: 300-400 years
  • Native to: Northeastern United States, Southeastern Canada
  • Leaf Shape: 5-lobed with deep sinuses

Sugar Maples require 40 years of growth before they can be tapped for maple syrup production.

Tree Conservation

Why Trees Matter

Trees are vital for biodiversity, carbon sequestration, soil health, and water cycle regulation. A single mature tree can absorb up to 48 pounds of carbon dioxide per year.

Threats to Our Forests

Deforestation, climate change, invasive pests, and urban development threaten tree populations worldwide. Over 15 billion trees are cut down annually.

What You Can Do

  • Plant native tree species in your yard or community
  • Support sustainable forestry by buying FSC-certified wood products
  • Volunteer for local tree planting initiatives
  • Learn to identify and report invasive tree pests and diseases
  • Advocate for tree protection policies in your community
